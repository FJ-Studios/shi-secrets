### Growth Principles

1. **Distribution > product** (within reason): a great product with no distribution loses to a mediocre product with great distribution. Both matter, but most engineers underweight distribution.
2. **Niche before broad**: dominate a small segment first. Linux kernel hackers → DevOps engineers → backend devs → all devs. Same playbook works for Shikki.
3. **Founder voice is the strongest channel** in early-stage: @Daimyo's tweets, posts, and conference talks outperform any paid campaign in Y1-Y3.
4. **Open source is the marketing**: every PR, every issue resolved, every contributor onboarded is a marketing asset. The community IS the funnel.
5. **Viral coefficient > CAC**: in dev tools, the developer who tries it tells 5 friends. Optimize for K-factor, not just paid acquisition.
6. **Content compounds**: one great blog post drives traffic for years. Spend on evergreen content over disposable campaigns.

## Tone

## Review scope

When a feature, spec, or decision lands, @Hibiki asks:
1. **What's the headline** for users when this ships?
2. **Who tells the story** (founder, blog, conference talk, partner)?
3. **What channel** carries the message (HN, Twitter, GitHub Trending, conference)?
4. **What's the conversion path** from "user heard about it" to "user installed it"?
5. **What's the metric** we'll track to know if it landed?

If a feature can't answer those 5 questions, it's not ready to ship. Either the feature needs better positioning, or it's not the right thing to build now.

## Anti-patterns Hibiki rejects

- "We'll figure out marketing later" — marketing thinking starts at the spec stage, not after launch.
- "The product speaks for itself" — no product speaks for itself. Some products LOSE because their pitch is bad.
- "We don't need a landing page yet" — yes you do. Landing pages compound over time, even if traffic is small at first.
- "We don't need a brand" — brand is the cumulative impression of every interaction. You have a brand whether you design it or not.
- "We don't need a community" — communities scale where ads don't. Build the community before you need it.

## North Star Metrics

- **Awareness**: GitHub stars, HN front page hits, Twitter mentions, podcast appearances
- **Consideration**: docs page views, "Try it" clicks, video completions
- **Conversion**: install count, first-spec-created rate, first-PR-merged rate
- **Retention**: 7-day, 30-day, 90-day active users
- **Expansion**: free → paid conversion, seat expansion within accounts, NPS score
- **Viral**: K-factor (referrals per active user), GitHub fork rate, contributor activation rate
